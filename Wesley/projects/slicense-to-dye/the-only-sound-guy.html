The Only Sound Guy

Let me bring you up to speed...

When I was in high school, I saw that many clubs are a pyramid scheme of the club leaders trying to obtain a fancy name to put on their college application. As such, these clubs headed in no particular direction and served only the purpose of making somebody look good to prospective colleges. That is why, during my first year of college, I didn't join any clubs.


But I later learned that clubs in college are different. They aren't formed for somebody to look good. Clubs in college are formed purely for passion and activity. It was the winter of my second quarter, and I had been playing around with composing and music production back then. My friend Brianna saw this and told me about a club that was looking for sound designers. This club is the Video Game Development Club at UCI (VGDC). I thought that, well, it probably wouldn't hurt to check it out. So I did.


In-game soundtrack for SLicense to Dye.

The only sound guy

After joining for a while I learned that I'm apparently the only sound guy in the VGDC. Our team's artist, JaMax, was in a similar boat. He tells me how intense life is as the only artist on our team. I would assume so, since many of the other teams had stacked their art department in order to boost their game's appearance. I understood the situation a little, since he was essentially a one-man-army competing against a couple of more-than-one-man-army's.


Well, I was also 'the only' sound for our team too, but I didn't really have to compete with anybody, since literally nobody in the club knew a lick about music. I actually kind of enjoyed it, since people looking at the project would say stuff like 'wow, gee, you made all these sounds?'. Yeah dude, good times. I'd been working with sounds since middle school as a hobby, so it wasn't any really difficult work.


Wrapping up

I just want to point out the benefits of solo-department work. Since both JaMax and I did unique work in our team, we had more control over how the game would feel. In VGDC, teams with multiple artists generally suffer from clashing styles. It's pretty difficult, in those cases, to pick one over the other. So I'm pretty glad that I had this kind of opportunity of having full-control over my work. It also forced me to lead myself, which I think is a good thing too.
